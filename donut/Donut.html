Donut Nutrition Facts

Warning: Eating too many donuts can be bad for your health. Just ask Winston.

A donut is a type of deep fried dough confectionery or dessert food. The doughnut is popular in many countries and prepared in various forms as a sweet snack that can be homade or purchased in bakeries, supermarkets, food stalls, and franchised specialty outlets. Did you know Donut can also be spelled "doughnut." The two most common types are the toroidal ring donut and the filled donut-which is injected with fruit preserves, cream, custard, or other sweet fillings. A small shperical piece of dough may be cooked as a donut hole. Other shapes include rings, balls, and flattened spheres, as well as ear shapes, twists, and other forms. Donut varieties are also divided into cake and risen type donuts.
